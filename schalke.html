Schalke 04: Restoring Royal Blue Dominance

A comprehensive Football Manager guide for rebuilding Schalke 04 and climbing back to Bundesliga greatness.

Squad Overview

Schalke 04 begin in the 2. Bundesliga but remain one of Germany's most historic clubs. The squad includes experienced veterans and emerging talent, but lacks depth in several key areas — especially defense and central midfield. Leadership and morale are mixed due to recent relegations.

Preferred Formation

A 4-2-3-1 Wide or 4-3-3 DM Wide offers a solid foundation. The team performs well in a structured, high-press system with quick transitions.

  • Goalkeeper: Focus on consistent shot-stopping — experience preferred.
  • Full-backs: Modern, overlapping full-backs add width to attacks.
  • Wingers: Inverted wingers suit players like Kenan Karaman or a signing like Kristijan Bistrović.
  • Striker: Use a Target Forward or Pressing Forward up top — Terodde fits this role well in early seasons.

Key Players

  • Simon Terodde: A goal machine in the 2. Bundesliga. Ageing, but crucial for promotion.
  • Thomas Ouwejan: Dynamic left-back with great crossing ability. Needs defensive cover.
  • Danny Latza: Veteran midfielder and club captain. Good for leadership but may need replacing soon.
  • Kenan Karaman: Versatile attacker who can play wide or centrally.

Transfer Recommendations

Incomings

  • Kristijan Bistrović (CM/CAM): On loan from CSKA. Make his move permanent if he fits your system.
  • Tom Rothe (LB - Loan): Great option to back up or rotate with Ouwejan.
  • Nnamdi Collins (CB - Borussia Dortmund): Great loan option or cheap buy with high upside.
  • Arvin Appiah (RW): Cheap, pacy option from Almería or on loan — adds directness to attack.

Outgoings

  • Marcin Kamiński: Consider moving on for younger, more mobile CBs.
  • Dominick Drexler: Ageing and not essential — could free wages.

Youth Prospects to Develop

  • Assan Ouédraogo: One of the best midfield talents in Germany — build around him!
  • Mehmet Aydin: Can develop into a solid right-back or wing-back.
  • Leo Greiml: Young defender with solid potential — worth giving minutes.

Tactical Tips

  • Use high pressing and gegenpressing against weaker sides.
  • Switch to cautious or balanced when facing top-tier sides or away games.
  • Encourage overlaps and attacking full-backs to create wide overloads.
  • Focus training on team cohesion — you’ll need strong chemistry to challenge for promotion.

Objectives

  1. Secure top 3 finish in first season, ideally promotion via top 2.
  2. Rebuild squad depth and reduce wage bill.
  3. Gradually integrate youth players like Ouédraogo.
  4. Develop a long-term tactical identity — fast, aggressive, and exciting football.
